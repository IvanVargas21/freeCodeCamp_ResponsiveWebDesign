Here's a time line of Quincy Larson's life:

  • 1955 - Quincy Larson was born on October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington, where he developed an early interest in technology and education.
  • 1973-1975 - Larson attended Harvard University, where he studied economics before pursuing a career in education as a teacher and school director in China.
  • 2000s - After returning to the U.S., Larson worked as a school director in California, gaining valuable experience in managing educational operations and curriculum development.
  • 2011-2013 - Recognizing the importance of technology, Larson taught himself to code using online resources and coding boot camps, preparing him for a new career path.
  • 2014 - In 2014, Larson founded freeCodeCamp to provide a free, accessible platform for coding education through hands-on projects and community support, which rapidly gained popularity.
  • 2015-2018 - freeCodeCamp's curriculum expanded to include a wide range of programming languages and data science topics, and Larson launched a blog and YouTube channel to further share coding knowledge.
  • 2019-2021 - During this period, freeCodeCamp officially became a nonprofit organization, focusing on global coding education and continuing Larson's advocacy for open-source learning.
  • 2022-Present - Larson remains actively involved in freeCodeCamp, working to enhance the platform and sharing his insights through articles and content on technology and education.
